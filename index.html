About Me

Industrial design graduate with a passion for XR technology, UX design, and entrepreneurship


Identity

My passion for technological innovation is rooted in the characteristic that I am always challenging myself to learn. I like a hands-on approach to learning a new skill, or to a design project. Programming and 3D realization are things that suit me well, and together with my passion for innovation, have led me to develop a healthy obsession for Extended Reality (XR*) design.

During my BSc I have developed myself in different complementary expertise areas to become a well-rounded designer. I want to change the world by realizing great products, but besides technological skills, this requires a good understanding of the design process, business, research and user-centered design.

I completed the ‘Technology Entrepreneurship’ trajectory, because I think combining entrepreneurship with innovative product design is the best combination to foster a positive impact on the world. With my skillset I want to achieve this through my own ventures, or by being entrepreneurial within a company.

I get a lot of motivation from collaborating in a team that works together to build a product. I like to collaborate with people from different expertise’s, because of how everyone brings their own knowledge and skills to the table. Being a designer and entrepreneur means taking leadership, so besides typical design tasks, my role would be to shape the product vision and use this to push the team forward.

Vision

With a future where technological advancements continue to evolve at an unprecedented rate, we will have to constantly rethink the connection between humanity and technology to let the two grow together. Just like the smartphone did in 2007, I think XR technology will cause a turning point in how users interact with technology, and in society as a whole.

Being learn-eager myself, I see immense potential in applying XR for education, allowing intelligent technology to help create more intelligent humans. I imagine a future where personalized, immersive, and interactive experiences will play a key role in an individual’s education. Despite this potential, the present-day raises challenges for designers and leaders to responsibly design the impact on the user and society.

The 'Metaverse' for example is going to be detrimental if the priorities of the users are not aligned with the priorities of the people creating it. It is my role as a designer to help create this with responsibility to the user, especially the most vulnerable. Because this challenge is so complex, it motivates me to keep developing myself to be able to do the best I can.

To design for the user on products that are evolving far past simple UI’s and simple functionalities, it takes an ever-evolving skillset to understand what the users’ needs actually are, and I think I have the potential to do so.

*Extended Reality (XR)
Extended reality (XR) is an umbrella term for any technology that alters reality by adding digital elements to the physical or real-world environment to any extent and includes, but is not limited to, augmented reality (AR), mixed reality (MR) and virtual reality (VR).

Type Travel VR

Worlds’ first typing course in VR


For my Final Bachelor Project I started the design of a VR course that teaches the player touch typing from start-to-finish. In the game, the player is brought along on a time travel adventure that tasks them to collect codes, save the inhabitants of the past, and beat the villain.

Image tracking technology is used to bring the hands and keyboard into VR. With custom hand- and keyboard visualizations the player is able to locate keys more effectively, and get less help as the course progresses. The course is designed for children aged 9-13.

Process

Type Travel VR is currently on iteration 3, working towards user research to test the effectiveness of the full course over a longer period of time. Prior to the current iteration stands a process of exploration, user research, and shipping a demo game on online platforms. The process is structured in iterations of 10 weeks:

With iteration 1, I created an artefact game that was used for play testing in 1-on-1 sessions with 20 children. This artefact acted as a proof of concept, and allowed me to effectively test the design of the interactions, story, and course content for improvements in the next iteration.

With iteration 2 I created a 12-minute demo game that showcases the design and unique mechanics. The game is released to the public to gather direct customer feedback and build excitement for the full course.

Structuring the process in iterations with clear deliverables created clear goals and (time) restrictions. This helped me in development phases to keep a focus on the essential features and stay motivated to work hard to reach the deadline. both iterations contain consequent phases of learning, designing, building, and testing.

Outcomes

The product is developed further as a start-up, but there were clear outcomes to my Final Bachelor Project. I scheduled iteration 2 to be completed by the time of the final deadlines. This includes the 12-minute demo game, and the business deliverables necessary to continue the project as a start-up.

Design internship at SAP

Design and development of XR environment for virtual production


At SAP, my main activity has been creating 3D environments that are used in an 'XR studio' to place a presenter or event host in a virtual environment (virtual production). Together with the team I realised many videos and live events for the company and its customers. Furthermore, I was entrusted to improve the usability- and deployability of the XR studio. The result of this process was a new XR presentation that demonstrates the SAP Strategy and its software products.

Process

To resolve usability issues in the IX studio, a good initial overview of the current pain points and opportunities was essential. The first step was interviewing the stakeholders of the studio. Findings from the 13 interviews were analysed and grouped in 9 categories to form the design goals.


After pitching 4 initial concepts based on the stakeholder interviews, my supervisor helped me choose the one that fit best with the priorities of the department. After this decision I started the process of designing environments and developing them in Unreal Engine.

This process consisted of creating a mood board, that was revised multiple times to come to a concession about the story, the general style, and the structure of the scenes.

Starting in Unreal Engine from early on allowed for exploration and a process of many iterations; each making the worlds more realistic, readable, and aesthetic.

Outcomes

The deliverable is an Unreal Engine project file with 8 unique scenes. Scene switches, animations, and the virtual screens can be controlled by the studio team through a control panel. This allows sales and pre-sales to present the SAP strategy and products in a more engaging way.

Besides the Unreal Engine project file, I created an instruction manual with guidelines for both the studio team and the presenter. This ensures that the story is structured in a coherent way and the technology is used properly.

HEXO

HEXO

Data collecting helmet for epilepsy patients


Together with 3 fellow students we set out to design a novel IoT device for healthcare. Through a process of many iterations a prototype was created. HEXO strikes a balance between being lightweight, breathable, strong, and safe. Not only does it protect the user, but it also measures the impact of a fall and identifies the location of the impact.

Process

Through primary desktop research, the limitations of epilepsy patients came up as an interesting topic that we based our design challenge on. Because of the vulnerable target user, the project was done in collaboration with experts in the field of neurology, epilepsy, and sleeping disorders. This started us off with a thorough understanding of the problems these people face, on a technical- and on a human level.

It took 5 iterations before the final concept was reached. These iterations consisted of ideation, low fidelity prototyping, and evaluation with experts and users. Feedback from the previous iteration was used to fuel the next iteration and come to a final concept that met the needs of all stakeholders. The most insightful evaluations were those with the users themselves because they were able to tell us exactly what their problems were.

To realize the final prototype, I was responsible for the design of the data visualisation and hosting a server to handle communication. Although the design of the app was only included in the last iteration, this sparked my passion to dive deeper into the UX design of software products.

Outcomes

The result of the project is a prototype; A 3D printed helmet with sensors and microcontroller, that is connected to an app.

With this prototype we competed in the TU/e contest where we reached the finals. We worked out a product roadmap and planned to collaborate with outside parties to develop a second prototype that would be suitable for heavy testing. Although this project has taught me a lot, I chose to disregard the project to focus on new opportunities.

Party Cube

Party game for remote play during COVID


Party Cube is designed to bring friends together in times of COVID. The game was creatd in a team with 2 other designers and 2 developers.

Process

Together with my team I researched the values that come about in multiplayer games and what makes them so much fun. This first phase involved ideation and the exploration of party games. (Including my personal favourites, Mario Party and Gang Beasts)

Seeing ourselves and our friends as target users made it easy to emphasise and create something new and fun. With a focus on this target user, I created a persona and sketched out level designs. The mini games were iteratively developed and tested. A lot of thought went into the scoring system and balancing the game in a fair and fun way. The game is developed in Unity, which was my first project using a game engine.

Outcomes

The outcome of this 10-week project was a functioning multiplayer game where friends could play together remotely. Although far from finished, it was already a fun experience to play mini games against friends and feel the sense of fun and excitement that we tried our best to design.

FriendsFirst

Social media platform for 1st year university students during COVID


Together with an interdisciplinary team ranging from computer science- to business students, I set out to create a Minimum Viable Product that was easy to conceptualise and test. FriendsFirst is a social media app that enables first year university students to meet each other and plan activities together.

Process

The product is created with the LEAN strategy to quickly test the concept with the target user. I created designed a mock-up app in Adobe Xd which was presented on a mock-up website with a call to action to download the app in the app store. The outcome of the project was a successful market study that suggests the potential of the product. 21% of target users tried to download the app, and the feedback from participants was generally positive.
